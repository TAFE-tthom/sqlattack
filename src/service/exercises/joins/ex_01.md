
## Joins - Actors and Films

Use an inner join between `Actor`, `Film_Actor` and `Film` and output the first name, last name of the actor and title of the film. Additionally, filter the results so only an actor that has the first name of `BURT`.

  * `first_name`, `VARCHAR`
  * `last_name`, `VARCHAR` 
  * `title`, `VARCHAR`

---

You will need to use the following information to help with forming your inner join.

* `actor`
  * `actor_id`, `INT`
  * `first_name`, `VARCHAR`
  * `last_name`, `VARCHAR`


* `film`
  * `film_id`, `INT`
  * `title`, `TEXT`
  * `description`, `TEXT`
  * `release_year`, `INT`
  * `language_id`, `INT`
  * `original_language_id`, `INT`
  * `rental_duration`, `INT`
  * `rental_rate`, `REAL`
  * `length`, `INT`
  * `replacement_cost`, `REAL`
  * `rating`, `CHAR`
  * `special_features`, `TEXT`

* `film_actor`
  * `film_id`, `INT`
  * `actor_id`, `INT`

---

Join statements will operate on multiple tables, you will need to ensure that you map the ids effectively between the table. Identify the ids and how the *joining tables* are used in this situation.
